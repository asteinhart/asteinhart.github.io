About me

I'm passionate about using data, design, and engineering to solve problems and tell stories that matter. I am currently the data visual editor at Council on Foreign Relations, where I work with reporters and editors to create compelling data-driven stories. I have an M.S. in Computational Analysis & Public Policy, a joint program by the Harris School of Public Policy and the Department of Computer Science at the University of Chicago. Previously, I was a research engineer with the Mansueto Institute for Urban Innovation working on topics related to public transpiration, property ownership networks, public finance, and property taxes and the data editor at the Chicago Maroon.

Before that I ran youth programming at Changeist and worked on the analytics team at the DNC. Find me on Linkedin or reach out via email at asteinhart3 at gmail.com
