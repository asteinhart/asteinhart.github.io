1. What is scrollytelling and when to use it

Scrollytelling, a portmanteau of "scrolling" and "storytelling", has become a popular technique to tell immersive, digitally native stories. Taking advantage of the now universal "scroll" pattern on a webpage, scrollytelling stories often include graphics and interactive elements in an effort to make a story more engaging and accessible

1.1. When to use scrollytelling

Traditional graphic techniques, such as inline graphics or interactive / personalized components are still the default style for most stories today. Scrollytelling can be a powerful tool for the right story. Stories that lend themselves to scrollytelling:

  • Have a strong narrative structure
  • Are inherently visual
  • Guide the readers step by step through a complex concept or process
  • Strong narrative arc with elements like a dramatic reveal or surprising twist.

Scrollytelling of course has its downsides as well.

  • They can be more complicated to produce than traditional articles, as it often requires more resources and expertise to create the graphics and interactive elements.
  • It can be less accessible to users with disabilities, as it may not be compatible with screen readers or other assistive technologies.
  • Elements designed for desktop may not translate well to mobile devices.

1.2. Types of scrollytelling

Scrollytelling could be considered one subset of a larger category of "immersive storytelling" techniques, also sometimes called "interactives." The most common scrollytelling technique is the "scroll-based steps" style, where cards of text are overlaid on a background and certain cards trigger animations or changes as the user scrolls. This is the type of scrollytelling that will be covered in this guide. However, there are many other types of immersive storytelling techniques that can be used to create engaging and interactive stories:

  • Scroll-based static: A vertically long static graphical element that goes beyond the viewport that the user must scroll through. (ex 1, ex 2)
  • Scroll-based progress: Animations connected to the progress of the users scroll down then pages (ex 1)
  • Click-based progression: A technique where the story progresses based on user clicks rather than scrolling. (ex 1 on mobile)
