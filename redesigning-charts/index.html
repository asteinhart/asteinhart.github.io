Redesigning My Old Charts with Adobe Illustrator and ai2html

Revising old work in an effort to improve my design and learn Adobe Illustrate and ai2html

By Austin Steinhart

Spring 2025

This spring I attended NICAR for the first time, a wonderful conference full of data journalist nerds. As someone new to the journalism world, I am constainlty looking to better understand the industry and what skills / tools are being used across the industry. One very common workflow I have observed to make graphics is to use a no/low code platform like Datawrapper or Flourish to make a chart, download as an SVG, upload into Adobe Illustrator, make edits and then use the lovely open source tool ai2html created by the New York Times to improve accessability, responsiveness and allow for easy simple interaction with Javascript. I was ecstatic to attend a session at NICAR called something that walked us through the process. I soon after scrounged my way onto someones Adobe account for Illustrator access and proceeded to watch three hours of Youtube tutorials on Illustrator and ai2html.

In an effort to get learn thes workflow as well as get some always helpful design practice, I redesigned some old charts I created or edited for the Chicago Maroon.

Here is the original chart from a story about the winning first year candidates at the UChicago College results. The article mentions that the number of candidates have declined since 2020. This chart is simple but not very pretty or descriptive. Lets try to fix it.
First, lets clean up the axises. We don't really need the y-axis label as its in the title and can remove the tick marks on the x-axis. following the advice of Tuft to try to remove as many lin e and elements as possible while still maintain clarity.
Next, there is no real need to have the circles in the line, those can be removed.
I believe graphics should provide the reader with the insight that the visual surfaces within the data. As mentioned in the article, we can see a decline in candidates from 2020 to 2023, reaching an 8-year low. Lets color and annotate the chart to surface this finding.
Lastly, lets update the title and subtitle to reflect this finding.
Just a few small changes made this look a lot better! Along with making the edits in Illustrator, the magic of ai2html also allowed me to control specific elements of the chart with Javascript which I used to remove and add specific elements in this example.
